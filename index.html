Why — The Problem

Indecision and overwhelm have become the defining experience of online shopping.

American consumers face thousands of near-identical products across hundreds of retailers, with contradictory reviews and prices that vary wildly. This creates decision fatigue — a well-documented phenomenon where too many options produce worse decisions and abandoned purchases.

A 2023 Accenture study found that 54% of US consumers feel overwhelmed shopping online and 91% have abandoned a purchase due to indecision. A McKinsey report estimates this costs American shoppers over $1.3 trillion annually in returns and missed value.

Our own primary research confirmed these findings: 72% of consumers regularly feel overwhelmed when shopping, and 96% have abandoned a cart before completing a purchase.

The root cause: today's platforms are built to serve merchants and advertisers, not consumers. No platform currently gives shoppers a single, trusted, personalized answer.

What — The Solution

One app. One answer.

JUVO is an AI-powered shopping assistant that eliminates decision fatigue by delivering one confident, personalized recommendation: the best-fit product at the best available price, every time.

Users connect existing shopping accounts — Amazon, Target, Walmart — and JUVO's machine learning analyzes their purchase history, preferences, and shopping behavior to build a personalized experience.

JUVO then searches across every major retailer in real time, scores each product on price and quality, and delivers a single recommendation tailored to that specific user.

4th Industrial Revolution Technology

JUVO is powered by artificial intelligence and machine learning — core 4IR technologies. Unlike static comparison tools that return generic rankings, JUVO's AI learns from each purchase and continuously refines its recommendations. What makes this defensible is the proprietary preference data JUVO builds over time for each user. The longer someone uses JUVO, the more accurate and personalized it becomes — creating a compounding advantage no competitor can easily replicate.

Who — Target Market & Size

The overwhelmed American consumer.

JUVO targets the overwhelmed American consumer, with an initial focus on new homeowners and young families. These groups are ideal: they face high purchase volume and high financial stakes, yet have limited time and budget to research every decision. Psychographic data from our primary survey revealed three defining traits:

  • Decision-fatigued browsers (72% of respondents): digitally active shoppers who browse multiple sites before buying yet still feel uncertain.
  • Convenience-driven (68% confirmed): willing to adopt tools that remove uncertainty and save time.
  • Personalization hungry: distrust generic best-seller lists and want recommendations grounded in their own behavior.

There are 228 million digital shoppers in the US (eMarketer, 2024). Targeting the 54% who experience decision fatigue yields a serviceable minimum market of 123 million consumers. Importantly, 93% of our surveyed potential users said they would probably or definitely use an app that recommends products based on their personal shopping history.

Competitive Advantage

While shopping assistants and comparison tools exist, none combine personalized AI recommendations with real-time, cross-platform price comparison. Google Shopping serves ads. Wirecutter publishes editorial picks. Honey finds coupons. None learn from individual behavior.

JUVO is the only retailer-agnostic platform that builds a personal preference profile and uses it to deliver a single trusted recommendation.

  • Only platform combining AI personalization, real-time price comparison, and quality scoring
  • Proprietary preference data compounds over time, creating a moat competitors cannot easily copy
  • Retailer-agnostic and free of ad-revenue incentives, giving users a source they can actually trust
How JUVO Makes Money

JUVO earns revenue through retailer affiliate commissions. When a user purchases through a partner retailer (Amazon, Walmart, Target, Best Buy), JUVO earns 2 to 8% of the sale value. This model has three strategic advantages:

  • Free for users: no paywall maximizes adoption, especially among budget-conscious families
  • Aligned incentives: JUVO only earns when users buy the right product at the best price, keeping goals fully consumer-aligned
  • Scales with engagement: revenue grows directly with purchase volume, not ad spend
